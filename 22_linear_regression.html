Empirical models

  • Relationship between two or more variables
    • Pressure of a gas in a container related to temperature
    • Water velocity in open channel related to width
  • \(d_t = d_0 + v t\) is a deterministic linear relationship
  • Regression analysis
  • One independent variable \(x\) and one dependent \(y\) with a linear relationship

Example

Simple linear regression model

\[E(Y|x)=\mu_{Y|x}=b_0 + b_1 x\]

\(b_0\): intercept and \(b_1\): slope are the regression coefficients

\[Y=b_0 + b_1 x + \epsilon\] is the simple linear regression model

\[E(Y|x)=E(b_0+b_1x+\epsilon)=b_0+b_1x+E(\epsilon)=b_0+b_1x\]

\[V(Y|x)=V(b_0+b_1x+\epsilon)=V(b_0+b_1x)+V(\epsilon)=0+\sigma^2=\sigma^2\]

Estimating the regression coefficients

\[Y=b_0+b_1x+\epsilon\]

\(\epsilon\) are assumed to be uncorrelated random errors with mean zero and unknown variance \(\sigma^2\)

\(n\) pairs of observations \((x_1,y_1)\), \((x_2,y_2)\),…, \((x_n,y_n)\)

\[y_i=b_0+b_1x_i+\epsilon_{i}\]

Least squares

Minimize the sum of squares of the vertical deviations (method of least squares)

\[L=\sum_{i=1}^{n}\epsilon_{i}^{2}=\sum_{i=1}^n (y_i-b_0-b_1x_i)^2\]

\[ \frac{\partial L}{\partial b_0} = -2 \sum_{i=1}^n \left( y_i-\hat{b}_0-\hat{b}_1x_i \right) = 0\]

\[\dfrac{\partial L}{\partial b_1} = -2 \sum_{i=1}^n \left( y_i-\hat{b}_0-\hat{b}_1x_i \right)x_i = 0\]

The least squares estimates of the slope and intercept are \[\hat{b}_0 = \overline{y} - \hat{b}_1 \overline{x}\] \[\hat{b}_1 = \dfrac{\sum_{i=1}^n y_i x_i - \dfrac{1}{n} \left(\sum_{i=1}^n y_i\right)\left(\sum_{i=1}^nx_{i}\right)}{\sum_{i=1}^n x_i^2 - \dfrac{1}{n} \left( \sum_{i=1}^n x_i\right)^2}\] where \(\overline{x}=(1/n)\sum_{i=1}^n x_i\) and \(\overline{y}=(1/n)\sum_{i=1}^n\)

The estimated regression line is therefore \[\hat{y}=\hat{b}_0+\hat{b}_1\] and each observation pair satisfies \[y_i=\hat{b}_{0}+\hat{b}_1+e_i\] where \(e_i=y_i-\hat{y}_i\) is the residual

Example

Fit a simple linear regression model to the oxygen purity data. From calculations of the \(n=20\) observations we have \(\sum x_i=23.92\), \(\sum y_i=1843.21\), \(\overline{x}=1.1960\), \(\overline{y}=92.1605\), \(\sum y_i^2=170044.5321\), \(\sum x_i^2=29.2892\), and \(\sum x_iy_i=2214.6566\).

Estimating \(\sigma^2\)

\[{SS}_E = \sum_{i=1}^n e_i^2 = \sum_{i=1}^n (y_i-\hat{y}_i)^2\]

\[{SS}_E = {SS}_T - \hat{b}_1 S_{xy}\] \[{SS}_T=\sum_{i=1}^n (y_i-\overline{y})^2 = \sum_{i=1}^n y_i^2 - n\overline{y}^2\]

\[\hat{\sigma}^2 = \dfrac{{SS}_E}{n-2}\]

Properties of least squares estimators

\[E(\hat{b}_1)=b_1, V(\hat{b}_1)=\dfrac{\sigma^2}{S_{xx}}\] \[E(\hat{b}_0)=b_0, V(\hat{b}_0)=\sigma^2 \left( \dfrac{1}{n} + \dfrac{\overline{x}^2}{S_{xx}} \right)\]

In simple linear regression, the estimated standard errors of the slope and intercept are \[{se}(\hat{b}_1) = \sqrt{\dfrac{\hat{\sigma^2}}{S_{xx}}}\] \[{se}(\hat{b}_0)=\sqrt{\hat{\sigma}^2 \left( \dfrac{1}{n} + \dfrac{\overline{x}^2}{S_{xx}} \right)}\]

Testing hypotheses for regression coefficients

We can test \(H_0: b_1=b_{1,0}\) and \(H_1: b_1 \neq b_{1,0}\)

\[T_0 = \dfrac{\hat{b}_1 - b_{1,0}}{\sqrt{\hat{\sigma}^{2} / S_{xx}}} = \dfrac{\hat{b}_1 - b_{1,0}}{{se}(\hat{b}_1)}\]

\[T_0 = \dfrac{\hat{b}_0 - b_{0,0}}{\sqrt{\hat{\sigma}^2 \left( \dfrac{1}{n} + \dfrac{\overline{x}^2}{S_{xx}} \right)}} = \dfrac{\hat{b}_0 - b_{0,0}}{{se}(\hat{b}_0)}\]

Example

Examine the regression for the oxygen purity data and evaluate its significance.

Confidence intervals on parameters

Under the assumption that the observations are normally and independently distributed, a \(100(1-\alpha)%\) confidence interval on the parameters is given by \[\hat{b}_1 - t_{\alpha/2,n-2} \sqrt{\dfrac{\hat{\sigma}^2}{S_{xx}}} \leq b_1 \leq \hat{b}_1 + t_{\alpha/2,n-2} \sqrt{\dfrac{\hat{\sigma}^2}{S_{xx}}}\] \[\hat{b}_0 - t_{\alpha/2,n-2} \sqrt{\hat{\sigma}^2 \left( \dfrac{1}{n} + \dfrac{\overline{x}^2}{S_{xx}} \right)} \leq b_0 \leq \hat{b}_0 + t_{\alpha/2,n-2} \sqrt{\hat{\sigma}^2 \left( \dfrac{1}{n} + \dfrac{\overline{x}^2}{S_{xx}} \right)}\]

Confidence interval on the mean response

A \(100(1-\alpha)%\) confidence interval on the mean response at the value of \(x=x_0\) (\(\mu_{Y|x_0}\)) is given by \[\hat{\mu}_{Y|x_0} - t_{\alpha/2,n-2} \sqrt{\hat{\sigma}^2 \left[ \dfrac{1}{n} + \dfrac{(x_0-\overline{x})^{2}}{S_{xx}} \right]} \leq \mu_{Y|x_0} \] \[ \leq \hat{\mu}_{Y|x_0} + t_{\alpha/2,n-2} \sqrt{\hat{\sigma}^2 \left[ \dfrac{1}{n} + \dfrac{(x_0-\overline{x})^{2}}{S_{xx}} \right]}\]
