Joint probability distributions

The probability distribution that defines the simultaneous behavior of two or more random variables

Joint probability mass function

The joint PMF of the discrete random variables \(X\) and \(Y\), denoted as \(f_{XY}(x, y)\), satisfies \[f_{X,Y}(x, y) \geq 0\] \[\sum_X f_{XY}(x,y) = 1\] \[f_{XY}(x,y) = P(X=x, Y=y)\]

Joint probability density function

A joint PDF for the continuous random variables \(X\) and \(Y\), denoted as \(f_{XY}(x,y)\), satisfies \[f_{XY}(x,y) \geq 0\] \[\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{XY}(x,y) dx dy = 1\] \[P[(X,Y) \in R] = \int \int_R f_{XY}(x,y) dx dy\]

Assume that the joint PDF is \[f_{XY}(x,y)=6 \times 10^{-6} exp(-0.001x-0.002y)\] What is the probability that the connection time will be less than 1000 ms and the authorization time will be less than 2000 ms?

Marginal probability distributions

If the joint PDF of random variables \(X\) and \(Y\) is \(f_{XY}(x,y)\), the marginal PDFs of \(X\) and \(Y\) are \[f_X(x)=\int f_{XY}(x,y)dy\] \[f_Y(y)=\int f_{XY}(x,y)dx\] where the first integral is for all \(Y\) and \(X=x\), and the second is for all \(X\) and \(Y=y\).

Mean and variance of a joint distribution

\[E(X) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x f_X(x) dx = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x f_{XY} dy dx\] \[V(X) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} (x - \mu_X)^2 f_X(x) dx = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} (x - \mu_X)^2 f_{XY}(x,y) dx dy\]

Conditional probability density function

Given continuous random variables \(X\) and \(Y\) with joint PDF \(f_{XY}(x,y)\), the conditional PDF of \(Y\) given \(X=x\) is \[f_{Y|x}(y) = \dfrac{f_{XY}(x,y)}{f_X(x)}\]

\[f_{Y|x}(y) \geq 0\] \[ \int f_{Y|x}(y) dy = 1\] \[P(Y \in B| X=x) = \int_B f_{Y|x}(y) dy\]

Conditional mean and variance

The conditional mean and variance of \(Y\) given \(X=x\) are \[E(Y|x) = \mu_{Y|x} = \int_y y f_{Y|x}(y) dy\] \[V(Y|x) = \sigma^2_{Y|x} = \int_y (y - \mu_{Y|x})^2 f_{Y|x}(x) dy\] \[= \int_y y^2 f_{Y|x}(y) dy - \mu^2_{Y|x}\]

Independence

If two random variables are independent, then for \(f_X(x)>0\) \[f_{Y|x}(y) = \dfrac{f_{XY}(x,y)}{f_X(x)} = \dfrac{f_X(x) f_Y(y)}{f_X(x)} = f_Y(y)\]

For random variables \(X\) and \(Y\), if one of the following properties is true the others are also true and \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent \[f_{XY}(x,y) = f_X(x) f_Y(y)\] \[f_{Y|x}(y) = f_Y(y) \text{ or } f_{X|y}(x) = f_X(x)\] \[P(X \in A, Y \in B) = P(X \in A)P(Y \in B)\]

Covariance and correlation

  • Apart from joint/conditional PDF we need additional measure of relationship between two random variables
  • Covariance and correlation
  • Expected value of a function of two random variables

\[E[h(X,Y)] = \begin{cases} \sum \sum h(x,y)f_{XY}(x,y) & X,Y\text{ discrete} \\ \int \int h(x,y) f_{XY}(x,y) dx dy & X, Y\text{ continuous} \end{cases}\]

Covariance

The covariance between the random variables \(X\) and \(Y\), denoted as \(\text{cov}(X,Y)\) or \(\sigma_{XY}\) is \[ \sigma_{XY} = E[(X - \mu_X)(Y - \mu_Y)] = E(XY) - \mu_x \mu_Y \]

Correlation

The correlation between random variables \(X\) and \(Y\), denoted \(\rho_{XY}\), is \[\rho_{XY} = \dfrac{\sigma_{XY}}{\sigma_X \sigma_Y}\]

For any two random variables \(X\) and \(Y\) \[-1 \leq \rho_{XY} \leq 1\]

Some caveats

  • Correlation only measures linear relationship between two variables!
  • Implications for causality
  • Always plot the data and check for outliers
